**Policy declaration text** is the summary page of an insurance policy. Think of it like the **receipt + ID card + quick facts sheet** for your coverage.

In auto insurance, the declaration page usually tells you **who is insured, what vehicle is insured, what coverage you bought, how much protection you have, and how much you pay.**

### Key concepts

**Named insured**
This is the main person or people covered by the policy.

Example:
PJ is listed as the named insured. That means the policy is officially under PJ’s name.

**Policy period**
This is the start and end date of the policy.

Example:
Coverage runs from **July 1, 2026 to January 1, 2027**. If an accident happens on August 5, it is inside the policy period. If it happens after January 1 and the policy was not renewed, coverage may not apply.

**Vehicle information**
This identifies the car covered by the policy.

Example:
2022 Toyota Camry, VIN ending in 1234.
If you crash a different car that is not listed, coverage may depend on the policy rules.

**Coverage types**
These are the kinds of protection you bought.

Example:
Liability coverage pays others if you injure them or damage their property. Collision pays for damage to your own car after a crash. Comprehensive pays for non-crash damage like theft, fire, hail, or vandalism.

**Coverage limits**
This is the maximum amount the insurance company will pay for a covered claim.

Example:
If your bodily injury liability limit is **$100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident**, the insurer may pay up to $100,000 for one injured person and up to $300,000 total for one accident.

**Deductible**
This is the amount you pay out of pocket before insurance pays.

Example:
You have a **$500 collision deductible**. Your car repair costs $3,000. You pay $500, and the insurer pays $2,500 if the claim is covered.

**Premium**
This is the price you pay for the policy.

Example:
Your six-month premium is **$900**. You might pay it all at once or in monthly payments.

**Endorsements or policy changes**
These are add-ons or changes to the standard policy.

Example:
Rental reimbursement, roadside assistance, gap coverage, or a rideshare endorsement for Uber/Lyft driving.

**Lienholder or loss payee**
This is usually the bank or lender that helped finance the car. They may have a right to payment if the car is damaged.

Example:
If Chase Auto Finance is listed as lienholder and the car is totaled, the claim payment may go partly or fully to Chase first.

**Garaging address**
This is where the car is normally kept.

Example:
If the car is mostly parked in New Jersey but the policy says it is garaged in Pennsylvania, that mismatch could cause problems.

### Simple way to think about it

The declaration text answers:

**Who is covered?**
**What car is covered?**
**When is it covered?**
**What kinds of damage or liability are covered?**
**How much will insurance pay?**
**How much do you pay first?**
**How much does the policy cost?**

For an AI insurance tool like PolicySight, the declaration text is important because it gives the tool the basic facts needed to explain coverage and evaluate a claim.

Here are a few **realistic but fictional auto insurance policy text samples** you can use for testing PolicySight. They are written to look like the kinds of text an AI agent may need to parse and explain.

## 2. Liability Coverage Sample

```text
PART A — LIABILITY COVERAGE

We will pay damages for bodily injury or property damage for which any insured becomes legally responsible because of an auto accident. We will settle or defend, as we consider appropriate, any claim or suit asking for these damages.

Our duty to settle or defend ends when our limit of liability for this coverage has been exhausted by payment of judgments or settlements.

Insured means:
1. You or any family member for the ownership, maintenance, or use of any auto or trailer.
2. Any person using your covered auto with your permission.
3. Any person or organization legally responsible for the acts or omissions of a person covered under this Part.

We do not provide Liability Coverage for any insured:
1. Who intentionally causes bodily injury or property damage.
2. For damage to property owned or being transported by that insured.
3. While using a vehicle without a reasonable belief that the insured is entitled to do so.
4. While using a vehicle to carry persons or property for a fee, unless this use is specifically covered by endorsement.
```

Good for testing: legal responsibility, defense duty, permissive use, exclusions, rideshare/delivery issue.

## 3. Collision Coverage Sample

```text
PART D — COVERAGE FOR DAMAGE TO YOUR AUTO

Collision means the upset of your covered auto or its impact with another vehicle or object.

We will pay for direct and accidental loss to your covered auto caused by collision, minus any applicable deductible shown in the Declarations.

The most we will pay for loss will be the lesser of:
1. The actual cash value of the stolen or damaged property;
2. The amount necessary to repair or replace the property with parts of like kind and quality; or
3. Any stated amount shown in the Declarations.

If repair costs exceed the actual cash value of the vehicle, we may declare the vehicle a total loss.

No deductible applies to glass repair if shown in the Declarations.
```

Good for testing: deductible, actual cash value, repair vs total loss, collision definition.

## 4. Comprehensive Coverage Sample

```text
OTHER THAN COLLISION COVERAGE

Other Than Collision means loss caused by events other than collision, including but not limited to:

1. Missiles or falling objects;
2. Fire;
3. Theft or larceny;
4. Explosion or earthquake;
5. Windstorm, hail, water, or flood;
6. Malicious mischief or vandalism;
7. Riot or civil commotion;
8. Contact with bird or animal;
9. Breakage of glass.

We will pay for direct and accidental loss to your covered auto, minus the deductible shown in the Declarations.

Loss caused by wear and tear, freezing, mechanical breakdown, road damage to tires, or gradual deterioration is not covered unless it results from a covered loss.
```

Good for testing: hail, theft, animal strike, vandalism, flood, wear-and-tear exclusion.

## 5. Rental Reimbursement Sample

```text
RENTAL REIMBURSEMENT COVERAGE

If you pay a premium for Rental Reimbursement Coverage, we will reimburse you for expenses incurred to rent a substitute vehicle when your covered auto is withdrawn from use for more than 24 hours due to a covered Collision or Other Than Collision loss.

Our payment is limited to the daily and maximum limits shown in the Declarations.

This coverage does not apply:
1. If the loss is not covered under this policy;
2. To fuel, mileage charges, security deposits, or optional insurance purchased from the rental company;
3. After your covered auto has been repaired, replaced, or we have offered payment for a total loss.
```

Good for testing: conditional coverage, daily limit, max days, covered-loss dependency.

## 6. Rideshare / Delivery Exclusion Sample

```text
EXCLUSION — TRANSPORTATION NETWORK OR DELIVERY USE

We do not provide coverage for any insured while your covered auto is being used to transport persons or property for compensation, including but not limited to rideshare, delivery, courier, or food delivery services.

This exclusion applies from the time the insured logs into a transportation network platform or delivery application until the insured logs out, unless coverage is provided by a specific endorsement shown in the Declarations.
```

Good for testing: Uber/Lyft/DoorDash scenarios, logged-in ambiguity, endorsement override.

---

## 7. Gap Coverage Sample

```text
AUTO LOAN / LEASE COVERAGE

If your covered auto is a total loss and you owe more on your loan or lease than the actual cash value of the vehicle, we will pay the difference, subject to the limits shown in the Declarations.

This coverage does not pay for:
1. Overdue loan or lease payments;
2. Late fees or penalties;
3. Extended warranties;
4. Credit life insurance;
5. Negative equity from a prior loan;
6. Any amount over 25% of the actual cash value of the vehicle.
```

Good for testing: total loss, loan balance, actual cash value, exclusions, negative equity.

## 8. Policy Cancellation Sample

```text
CANCELLATION

You may cancel this policy by returning it to us or by giving us advance written notice of the date cancellation is to take effect.

We may cancel this policy by mailing notice to the named insured shown in the Declarations at the last known address.

If this policy has been in effect for less than 60 days, we may cancel for any reason permitted by law.

If this policy has been in effect for 60 days or more, we may cancel only for:
1. Nonpayment of premium;
2. Suspension or revocation of a driver’s license;
3. Material misrepresentation;
4. Fraudulent conduct related to this policy or a claim.
```

Good for testing: cancellation rights, notice, nonpayment, fraud, misrepresentation.
